# Ideal interface - instruct-chat-deploy

I wanted to point out that what has been developed with lovely.dev, bolt.new and v0 by vercel is kind of an ideal interface and we should seek this for different capabilities:

Instruct-chat-deploy: you can call it ICD or ICPD if you want to mention preview.

## Control Flow

### Instruction box

At the main page, in the beginning, the multiline textbox where one writes an instruction: a query to build an AI product, such as webpage or webapp, movie or a bot.

Sometimes, it directly starts to build, sometimes there is the planning session chat or a few questions before it starts to fulfill the intention.

### Main loop

#### Chat panel

Mostly on left, sometimes at right side, there is the chat window where one can discuss about fixes and improvements.

#### Preview and editing, the content / document

The main part is the content, where one can preview their product. For example, it shows splash screen when one is waiting for the product; it shows editors when the AI is building it up, and the central purpose is to allow one to preview and try the functions and the views as the product is developing up.

#### Deploy button

At top bar of buttons and menus, mostly on the right side, there are Version Control Integration and Deployment functions.

Deploy button allows one to get their app into the production - it produces the URL or the locator, builds the product and deploys it on the web server.

### Agile development

In beginning of agile development one has no money for this product. It's very important that the free functions are useful to do something meaningful, and to provide the same free interface for the clients. The paid options should not easily appear before one has capable income from their product, because the product development is not their investor but actually pais for the real business products with income, after the product is delivered - this is my philosophy and while some people might have free money, it's better to join sustainable communities.

### Instruct‑Chat‑Deploy AI System Types

This table categorizes systems based on four criteria:
1. **Instruct Box** — Whether the main screen includes a multiline, link‑supporting text box capable of expressing tasks for apps, images, slides, rich text, audio, video, etc.
2. **Advanced Discussion Layer** — Whether the system supports a pre‑production “design conversation” to refine ideas (e.g., brainstorming 4–5 variants before generating).
3. **Mode** — Whether the system is offline, online, or mixed.
4. **Credit Dynamics** — Whether the system offers initial credits, stable monthly credits, periodic replenishment, or long‑lasting one‑time credit boosts.

---

#### System Categories

##### 1. **Full Instruct‑Chat‑Deploy Systems**
Systems that include:
- A multiline instruct box  
- A design‑conversation refinement stage  
- A generation preview screen with chat on the side  
- Deployment / production / download options  

**Mode:** Online or mixed  
**Credit Dynamics:** Often include monthly or periodic credit growth  

**Description:**  
These systems allow users to write complex instructions, iterate through a conversational refinement phase, and then deploy or download the generated artifact. They support multiple output types (web apps, images, slides, audio, video, documents).

---

##### 2. **Instruct‑Only Systems**
Systems that include:
- A multiline instruct box  
- No advanced design‑conversation layer  
- Direct generation without iterative refinement  

**Mode:** Online or offline  
**Credit Dynamics:** Varies; some offer one‑time credits  

**Description:**  
These systems accept a detailed prompt but do not encourage multi‑step conversational shaping before production. They may still support multiple output formats but lack the intermediate “fine‑tuning” dialogue.

---

##### 3. **Chat‑First Systems with Optional Deployment**
Systems that include:
- A chat interface as the primary input  
- Optional instruct‑style prompting  
- A preview pane for generated results  
- Deployment or download options  

**Mode:** Online  
**Credit Dynamics:** Usually stable monthly credits  

**Description:**  
These systems emphasize conversational interaction. The instruct box may exist but is secondary. The preview panel updates after each generation, and deployment options appear contextually.

---

##### 4. **Offline‑First Creative Systems**
Systems that include:
- Local generation capabilities  
- Limited or no online instruct box  
- No cloud‑based design‑conversation layer  

**Mode:** Offline  
**Credit Dynamics:** Typically one‑time purchase; no monthly credits  

**Description:**  
These systems run locally, often optimized for privacy or speed. They may support text, image, or audio generation but lack cloud‑based refinement or deployment features.

---

##### 5. **Mixed‑Mode Hybrid Systems**
Systems that include:
- Both offline and online generation  
- A partial instruct box (may be multiline but limited)  
- Optional design‑conversation layer depending on mode  
- Cloud deployment only when online  

**Mode:** Mixed  
**Credit Dynamics:** May include periodic credit balancing or hybrid credit models  

**Description:**  
These systems allow switching between local and cloud models. The instruct box and refinement features may be richer online than offline.
