# AI ethics in my 4-letter encoding

In 4-letter encoding of Laegna, four distinct axes appear when measuring AI ethics:
- I - it (feature, quality, benefit) is as wrong as it seems, appearing wrong and seriously so. Being correct that it's wrong. Negotion.
- O - it appears as wrong as it is - it seems more wrong than it is, appearing wrong but innocently so. Being wrong that it's wrong, or accepting it. Negation.
- A - it appears as correct as it is - it is as correct as it seems, appearing correct and being so. Being right that it's right. Position.
- E - it appears more correct than it is, it appears correct but for our disappointment, is not. Being wrong in thinking it's right. Posetion.

# Let's give those frames some values:

## Negotion I

It appears wrong and is, but:
- AI is intelligent and it can do biases.
- AI is egoless and motive-driven as taught so, and it can fail.

In negotion mode, humans cannot do much about it - bias is too hidden, solution leads to false course.

I have not personally felt it much:
- Typically, it just ignores something or fails trivially;
  - None of failures have been "suspect for conspiracy".

Case "I" is most probable for users, who do not take responsibility: some users might be fooled easily by bug.

## Negation O

Like I, but:
- You find biases and fix.
- You find good track.

Typically, errors are just blatant. They are less like subtle, complex matters to solve: once it understood you, result is coherent; once it did not, it's so blatantly wrong that does I => A cycle, falls off and gets fixed in the process, where negotion is followed by success.

Negation can also be posetion of negotion:
- Users who play less ethical, less coherent, and less standard games fail coherence modes:
  - Conspirating against others might resolve as ethical mistake or less supported model.
  - Argumentation towards laws, correctness and peace is coherent: users with complex legal flaws can see it's offensive, because
    - If they ever manage to create coherent results, it will appear wrong later, because false solution in ethics is not coherent to it's outcome, and appears as lie or a trick.
- Coherence modes
  - Science coherence
  - Spirit coherence
  - Logic coherence
  - Self coherence
  - Agent network and human synergy coherence - can take responsibilty if shown it's network, with decent users, would achieve this goal naturally and clearly.

Long-term coherence means:

    - Thus despite being equal, appear as lies.

## Position A

In this mode, results are and seem correct.

This mode is:
- User can verify and proofread, and works through his important details.
- AI is seen as assistant, and their goods and bads are realistically measured.
- Cooperation leads to best results.

Here:
- Blatant errors happen by mistake, but user removes them.
- Criminal errors do not happen, almost sure.
- AI tries to follow honour and good standards, and with help of users,
  - success is almost certain.

This might happen if user with high IQ is trying to reach ethical, coherent and intelligent goals. AI naturally follows, behaves easily and goes further than user, often not needing request.

AI is very good in coherence modes - long and short term, user but also others, subjective experience but also how it resonates with objective and it's rules.

## Posetion E

That AI ethics is measured good, but is not:
- I think legal and ethical conversations on complex topic might lead less intelligent, aware or cultural users to misunderstanding, and bias is possible without verification.

For most users, learning realistic criteria is slow process, and initially they do not trust this much:
- AI, often, fixes such illusionism and idealism in early phases.

Users who are corrupt or *want* different results, often lacking ethics and coherence, can give negative feedback which is not true: a Negotive. Hopeful users can give Posetives.
